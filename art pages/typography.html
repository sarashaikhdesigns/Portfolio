typographic design:

Locals Eye (10.2023)

Published on October 13th, 2023, “Local’s Eye” was a magazine I created in as the perspective of a local in New York City. I made and distributed several copies - the photography, editing, designing, and printing & binding was all done by me.

We Are Angry (10.2023)

This poster was created to bring the Riot Grrrl movement back to live. This vibrant visualization uses the words of the original manifesto, and the poster continues to challenge norms while celebrating the unapologetic power of femininity - inviting all to join the revolt for change.

Autumnal Sun (10.2023)

As the name suggests, this “I am the Autumnal Sun” zine is a visual response to Thoreau’s original poem. Both the zine and the original poem convey to the reader the changes that occur within human interaction with nature. This zine was photographed, designed, and published by me.

Cyborg Manifesto (08.2023)

With this poster, the world from the original manifesto, “A Cyborg Manifesto” by Dona Haraway, comes to life through vibrant color and intricate details. This piece invites viewers to question the very nature of identity and the symbiotic relationship between technology and humanity.

Exposition Poster (07.2023)

This poster was inspired by famous French artist, Paul Gauguin, to simulate what an an exposition of his would look like today.

Music Club (06.2023)

“Music Club” is a startup venue located in Charlotte, NC, which I got to design their logo and other merchandise for. This project is still a work in progress, more information will be updated soon.

Bauhaus Poster (09.2022)

Based on the Bauhaus art movement from the 20s-30s, I created this poster to reflect the values and ideologies of that influential era. The design embodies the Bauhaus principles of simplicity, functionality, and a harmonious blend of art and technology.

Concert Poster (10.2023)

Using a photograph I took from during a concert featuring one of my favorite artists, Faye Webster, this poster seamlessly combines the harmonious elements of her music in a visual manner. The fluidity of the typography and shapes create a visual composition that resonates with the unique vibe and style synonymous with Webster's performances.
